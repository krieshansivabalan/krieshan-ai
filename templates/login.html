Questions & Answers

Tropical astrology (used by most Western horoscope apps) fixes the zodiac to the seasons — 0° Aries always lands at the Spring Equinox, regardless of where the stars actually are. Sidereal astrology tracks the actual constellations. Because Earth's axis wobbles over time (a process called precession), the tropical and sidereal zodiacs have drifted about 23° apart. Krieshan AI uses the Lahiri ayanamsa — the official correction factor adopted by the Indian government — to place every planet where it actually sits in the sky at the moment of your birth. For most people born between 1970–2000, this shifts their Sun sign back by one full sign.
Most popular astrology apps use tropical Western astrology and AI-generated horoscopes. Krieshan AI is built from the ground up on Jyotish (Vedic astrology) using sidereal placements. We calculate divisional charts (D9, D10) that most apps don't offer at all. Our Soul Profile and archetype mapping are unique to this platform. And our upcoming AI Chart Guide will know your actual chart — not just generic sign descriptions. If you've ever felt that Western astrology didn't quite fit, the sidereal chart is often why.
The Navamsa (D9) is the most important divisional chart in Vedic astrology after the birth chart itself. Each sign is divided into nine equal parts of 3°20′, and the planets are redistributed into a new chart based on which navamsa they occupy. The D9 reveals the soul's deeper nature, the quality of your long-term relationships and marriage, and the way your chart will unfold in the second half of life. Many Jyotish astrologers check the Navamsa before making any predictions — a planet that is weak in the D1 but strong in the D9 is often considered a hidden strength, and vice versa.
The Dasamsa (D10) divides each sign into ten equal parts of 3°00′ each. It is the divisional chart dedicated to career, profession, and public life. Where your D1 shows your personality and life path broadly, the D10 zooms in on the quality of your professional work — the sector you're drawn to, how authority figures perceive you, your relationship with hierarchy, and the karmic purpose embedded in your vocation. Planets placed strongly in the D10 tend to confer worldly success in their domains; weak or afflicted planets here can indicate obstacles or delays in career.
